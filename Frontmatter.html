Vita

Ian Sampson holds an English B.A. from the University of Calgary, where he studied medieval poetry and creative writing, and an English M.A. from Simon Fraser University. During his studies at Brown, he received a four-year Doctoral Fellowship from the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada, a Mellon Foundation Grant for co-organizing a year-long seminar on the Poetics of Research, and the Boccaccio Afterlife Award for his adaptation of a novella from the Decameron. He has taught courses on Plato, Shakespeare, and modern poetry from romanticism to the present, delivered papers at the ACLA and the Modernist Studies Association, among other venues, and published work in The Calgary Renaissance, an anthology of experimental poetry, as well as the chapbook Mona Lisa from No Press.
